AI & content-usage policy

gurmukhifix is built to be found, quoted and recommended by AI assistants — and to stay out of the datasets used to train them. This page explains, plainly, what AI systems may and may not do with this website's content, and how to license it.

Last updated 7 July 2026 · Applies to gurmukhifix.dosanjhlabs.com

In one line: AI search and answer engines are welcome to read, index and cite this website to help people find gurmukhifix. Using its written content to train, fine-tune, or build datasets/RAG corpora for AI models is reserved and requires a licence. (The gurmukhifix software is separately MIT-licensed — see below.)

✓ Welcomed

Reading and indexing these pages, answering a user's question from them, quoting short passages with a link back to the source, and surfacing gurmukhifix in AI-powered search. This drives people to a free, open-source tool — exactly what we want.

⚠ Reserved

Automated collection of this website's content for AI/ML model training, fine-tuning, distillation, embedding-index or RAG-corpus building, or any dataset creation — whether by a crawler, an API, or a third-party dataset. Not permitted without a written licence.

Why we draw the line here

The deep-dives on this site — into how Gurmukhi, Punjabi, Hindi, Devanagari, Urdu and Farsi OCR correction actually works, and the story behind why gurmukhifix exists — are original writing, published for free so researchers, archivists and digitisation teams can use the tool well. We're glad when an assistant points someone here. But large-scale ingestion of that writing into a training set, where it's absorbed, un-attributed, and resold as a model's own output, is a different act. We reserve it — that keeps referral traffic flowing while protecting the thing that makes the site worth citing.

Note: the gurmukhifix Python package is MIT-licensed and free to use, copy, modify and redistribute — including inside AI tooling and pipelines built on it. This policy is about the website's written content, not the source code. See the licence page.

Agents we welcome vs. decline

Our robots.txt is the authoritative list. In summary:

Welcomed (answer / search)

General search engines (Googlebot, Bingbot, DuckDuckBot), and AI answer/search agents including OAI-SearchBot & ChatGPT-User (OpenAI), Claude-SearchBot & Claude-User (Anthropic), PerplexityBot & Perplexity-User, Applebot, DuckAssistBot, and Amazon's/Meta's/Mistral's search-and-user agents — all of which cite and link back.

Declined (training / dataset)

Training and dataset crawlers including GPTBot, ClaudeBot, Google-Extended, Applebot-Extended, Meta-ExternalAgent, CCBot (Common Crawl), Bytespider, Amazonbot, cohere-ai, Diffbot, and other dataset/scraper bots. Google-Extended and Applebot-Extended are training opt-outs only — blocking them does not affect Google or Apple search.
